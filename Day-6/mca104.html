The first-year MCA syllabus for Operating Systems typically covers the fundamental concepts and principles of operating systems, including process management, memory management, file systems, and I/O management. It also explores different types of operating systems, system calls, and the overall structure and components of an OS. Additionally, the syllabus often includes topics like deadlocks, concurrency, and synchronization, along with an introduction to popular operating systems like Unix/Linux and Windows.
